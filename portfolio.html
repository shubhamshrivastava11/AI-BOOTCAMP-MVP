Problem Statement

The $15,000 gap in home styling

95% of homeowners can't afford a professional interior designer, yet every existing alternative forces them to choose between inspiration without action, or endless options without context.

😱

Too expensive to hire

Professional designers charge $2,000–$15,000 per room. Havenly and Houzz are digital but still premium, pricing out the majority of new homeowners.

📷

Inspiration without purchase

Pinterest and Instagram create desire but offer no path to buy. Users save thousands of images and still don't know what to actually order or where to get it.

🛒

Options without curation

Wayfair and Amazon list 50,000+ sofas with no design context. Users spend hours comparing products that may not work together or match their style.

🏠

Single-brand lock-in

IKEA is affordable but limited to one aesthetic. Users who want a layered, eclectic, or premium look can't build a cohesive room from one retailer alone.

User Journey & Pain Points

From "I need furniture" to "Add to cart"

Five steps replacing what used to take weeks of research and thousands in consultation fees.

1

Set Your Budget

Enter a total room budget. Dwelliq automatically allocates it across categories using design industry ratios.

Pain: "I have $3,000 but have no idea how to split it across a sofa, rug, lighting, and tables."
2

Pick Your Style

Choose from 5 curated directions: Warm Mid-Century, Scandinavian Minimal, Earthy Organic, Bold Maximalist, or Industrial Loft.

Pain: "What even is 'Japandi'? I like what I like but I can't name it."
3

Describe Your Room

Answer 4 quick questions: room type, household size, pet situation, and existing anchor pieces.

Pain: "Every design app asks 40 questions and still gives generic results."
4

Get Recommendations

Receive 3 curated options per furniture piece: A (on-budget), B (if flexible), C (local boutique).

Pain: "I found the perfect sofa online but it doesn't ship here and nothing else matches."
5

Click & Buy

Each item links directly to the retailer via affiliate URL. No registration required. The list persists in session.

Pain: "I finally decided but closed the tab. Now I can't find those products again."

Features & Demo

Six core capabilities that close the gap

Each feature was designed around a specific drop-off point in the traditional home furnishing journey.

AI Core
📈

Smart Budget Allocation

Enter one number. The engine splits it using interior design industry ratios — 35% sofa, 20% case goods, 15% textiles, 10% lighting, 10% accent. Live tracker updates as you swap items.

✓ Prevents the most common decorating mistake: overspending on one hero piece
Core
🆕

A/B/C Three-Option System

Every furniture piece returns exactly 3 options: A is best-for-budget, B is best-if-flexible (+20–30%), C is best-local (independent boutique sourced by zip code). No endless scrolling.

✓ 3 decisions vs. 50,000 Wayfair results
UX
🏭

Style Direction Matching

Five curated aesthetic directions each map to a subset of the 200-SKU catalog. Warm Mid-Century, Scandinavian Minimal, Earthy Organic, Bold Maximalist, Industrial Loft — each with visually distinct product selections.

✓ 5 styles × 10 categories × 3 tiers = structured, not overwhelming
Core
📍

Local Boutique Finder

Option C surfaces independently-owned furniture stores within reasonable distance, creating a discovery layer for local commerce that Amazon and Wayfair structurally exclude.

✓ Differentiates Dwelliq from any purely national-retail aggregator
UX
💰

Real-Time Budget Tracker

Every item swap updates a persistent total in the header. The tracker shows category sub-totals and turns red when a selection would exceed the allocated category budget.

✓ Immediate feedback loop prevents post-session sticker shock
Core
🏠

Zero-Friction, Always Free

No account creation. No email capture. Results persist in sessionStorage for the duration of the browsing session. Revenue model is pure affiliate — Dwelliq earns only when users buy.

✓ Affiliate model aligns product incentives with user success

⚡ Zero Cold Start

By keeping all recommendation logic client-side in src/lib/recommendations.ts and the full catalog in memory, every recommendation is instant — no API round-trip, no loading spinner on the results page.

🔒 No Auth Surface

sessionStorage instead of a user database eliminates a major privacy and security concern for an MVP. Users get full functionality with zero friction. A user account layer can be added later when retention data justifies it.

📈 Revenue-Aligned Architecture

Every SKU in the catalog carries an affiliate URL. The system earns only when users click out to buy — perfectly aligning product incentives with user success. No subscription to cancel, no freemium wall to hit.
